Digital Payment Growth in Nepal: What's Next

Aayush Pokhrel
June 24, 2025
8 min read

In the past few years, Nepal has experienced a digital revolution in how people pay, receive, and manage money. From QR codes in local tea shops to digital wallets used by millions, the rise of digital payment systems has changed the financial landscape of the country. But what's driving this growth? And more importantly what comes next?

Rapid Growth in Numbers

Nepal's digital payments sector has witnessed remarkable growth:

374 Billion
Mobile banking transactions per month in NPR

Mobile banking transactions in Nepal have indeed crossed NPR 374 billion per month, reflecting a significant increase in digital payment usage. This growth is attributed to the rising accessibility of digital payment platforms and the increasing number of users adopting mobile banking services.

23 Million+
E-wallet users in Nepal

The number of e-wallet users in Nepal has significantly increased, with a reported growth of 23.86% in the last fiscal year, reaching over 23 million users. This surge reflects the growing popularity of digital payments and the shift towards a cashless economy.

230%
Annual growth in QR code transactions

QR-based transactions in Nepal have seen remarkable growth, with an average annual increase of 230% in transaction volume and 210% in transaction value from fiscal years 2021-22 to 2023-24. This surge is attributed to increased access to digital payment platforms and a shift towards cashless transactions.

These figures reflect how digital platforms have become part of everyday life from paying utility bills to buying groceries.

What's Driving the Growth?

1. Government & NRB Policies

The Nepal Rastra Bank (NRB) And The Nepalese government has been supportive of digital payment initiatives, promoting financial inclusion and the use of technology in the financial sector. Policies aimed at enhancing digital infrastructure and encouraging fintech innovations have contributed to this growth.

2. Widespread Smartphone Use

Smartphone use in Nepal is widespread, with over 73% of the population using smartphones as of 2025. This high penetration rate reflects the growing accessibility of mobile technology across various regions of the country.

3. Fintech Innovation

Companies like eSewa, Khalti, IME Pay, and Fonepay have made payments fast, easy, and mobile-friendly. They offer cashback, bill payments, and services that attract youth and rural users.

4. Cross-Border Payment Links

In 2024, Nepal integrated with India's UPI, enabling QR-based payments by Indian tourists. Fonepay and Lankapay is a strategic partnership to revolutionize the digital payment landscape by developing a cross-border digital payment system that enables users to transact seamlessly across the two countries Nepal and Srilanka.

Remaining Challenges

Despite success, a few challenges remain:

Challenge Description
Digital Literacy Many rural users still lack understanding of online payments
Cybersecurity Concerns Incidents of delays and weak fraud protection are rising
Cash Culture Many merchants and users still prefer cash
Infrastructure Gaps Internet and power outages hinder adoption in remote areas

What's Next for Digital Payments in Nepal?

1. CBDC (Digital Nepal Rupee)

The Rastra Bank (NRB) is actively working towards introducing a Central Bank Digital Currency, marking a significant step in modernising the nation's financial landscape. A pilot launch is targeted for 2026, with the system designed to support both wholesale and retail transactions. This will enhance financial inclusion, reduce dependency on physical cash and improve the transparency and efficiency of payments.

2. Digital-Only Banks

Digital only Bank refers to those banking services which are conducted entirely online without any physical branches. NRB plans to license neo-banks in 2025/26 these will be fully online with no branches. Which means user can manage their financial activities through website and apps.

3. Fintech Consolidation

IME Pay and Khalti are merging to compete against market leader esewa. The merger of IME Pay and Khalti aims to create a stronger competitor against eSewa, which currently dominates the digital wallet market in Nepal with nearly 70% market share. This consolidation is expected to enhance services and foster competition in the fintech sector.Which could lead to more features, better security, and reduced service charges.

4. Rural Expansion

Offline payment options (e.g., USSD services) and partnerships with telcos (like Ncell-Khalti) are growing. Literacy campaigns and micro-ATMs will help expand financial access.

5. Global Interoperability

Future steps may include accepting ASEAN QR codes, expanding remittance APIs, and global fintech collaboration.

Future Prospects

The future of digital payments in Nepal looks promising, with ongoing investments in technology and infrastructure. As more people become comfortable with digital transactions, and as the government continues to support fintech development, the digital payment landscape is expected to evolve further, potentially leading to greater financial inclusion and economic growth.

Conclusion

Nepal's journey from cash to clicks is just beginning. With supportive policy, innovation from fintechs, and increasing public trust, the future of digital payments in Nepal is bright. However, true inclusion will depend on how well these services reach the rural and underserved. As the country moves toward CBDC, digital banking, and cross-border integration, Nepal could soon become a digital payments leader in South Asia.

In summary, the digital payment ecosystem in Nepal is rapidly expanding, driven by technological advancements, changing consumer preferences, and supportive government policies. However, addressing existing challenges will be crucial for sustaining this growth and ensuring that it benefits the entire population.
