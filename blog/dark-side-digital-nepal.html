The Dark Side of Digital Nepal: Scams, Fraud, and How to Stay Safe Online

Aayush Pokhrel
July 6, 2025
10 min read

Over the past decade, Nepal's rapid digital transformation has brought big opportunities from online banking to e-governance, but it has also opened the floodgates to cybercriminals. Online scams, fraud and cybercriminals are increasingly targeting unsuspecting users in their everyday lives.

23.7 Million
Mobile banking users in Nepal

According to the Cyber Bureau, reports of online scams and fraud have been rapidly increasing. With 90% internet penetration and 23.7 million mobile banking users, Nepal is a prime target for scammers exploiting digital vulnerabilities. From phishing scams to AI-powered deepfakes, online scammers/cybercriminals are evolving faster than defenses. In 2024 alone, thousands of people lost their savings, personal data or even their dignity to scammers.

Common Online Scams in Nepal

1. Phishing Scams

Cybercriminals send fake emails or SMS messages pretending to be from banks, telecom companies, or popular apps like eSewa or Ncell. These often contain links that lead to fraudulent websites designed to steal login credentials or personal information.

Example: A message claiming your account is suspended and asking you to click a link to "verify" your details.

2. Fake Job Offers

Scammers post attractive job offers on social media or job portals, promising high salaries or quick money. They may ask victims to pay processing fees or provide sensitive information before disappearing.

  • No official contact details
  • Asking for payment upfront
  • Too-good-to-be-true salary offers

3. Online Shopping Frauds

With the rise of e-commerce, many fake online shops have emerged selling products at suspiciously low prices. After receiving payment, they either deliver counterfeit items or nothing at all.

Platforms used: Facebook Marketplace, Instagram, TikTok, and unknown local websites

4. Loan & Investment Scams

Apps or websites offering easy loans or investment schemes promise high returns or zero-interest loans but require upfront fees or access to your bank account.

Recent trend: Fake crypto or forex investment platforms targeting young investors.

5. SIM Swap Fraud

Criminals trick telecom providers into transferring a victim's phone number to a new SIM card, allowing them to bypass two-factor authentication and take control of bank accounts or digital wallets.

6. Social Media Hacking & Impersonation

Hackers gain access to Facebook, Instagram, or WhatsApp accounts and impersonate victims to scam friends and family.

7. Phishing & Fake Banking Calls

Scammers pretend to be from banks or mobile wallet companies, tricking users into sharing OTPs, PINs, or account details.

Real-Life Cases in Nepal

In 2023, hundreds of users lost thousands of rupees after falling for fake eSewa verification links. Many more have been victims of similar scams across various platforms.

Why Are These Scams So Common?

  • Low Digital Literacy: Many people don't know how to validate online information
  • Weak Regulation: Cybercrime laws exist but enforcement is slow and inefficient
  • Anonymity: Scammers easily hide behind fake profiles and untraceable phone numbers
  • Trust Culture: Many Nepalis are naturally trusting and hesitate to suspect fraud

How To Stay Safe Online in Nepal

1. Think Twice Before Clicking Any Link

Don't trust suspicious links or offers that sound too good to be true. Verify pages before making payments or sharing information.

2. Never Share OTPs or PINS

Your bank or e-wallet will NEVER ask for them. If you get such a call, hang up immediately.

3. Be Cautious with Personal Photos

Don't share sensitive photos or videos that could be misused.

4. Enable Two-Step Authentication

Wherever possible, enable 2FA on your email, banking, and social media accounts for an extra layer of security.

5. Use Strong, Unique Passwords

Avoid using simple passwords like "123456" or "password." Use a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols, and consider using a password manager.

6. Be Skeptical of Too-Good-to-Be-True Offers

If something seems too cheap or too easy, it probably is. Always verify the source before purchasing.

7. Report Suspicious Activity

If you suspect you've been scammed:

  • Contact your bank immediately
  • Report to Cyber Crime Bureau Nepal Police
  • File a complaint via cybercrime.gov.np
7 Essential
Cybersecurity practices for Nepali internet users
"Awareness is the First Line of Defense. While the government and private sector are working to improve cybersecurity infrastructure in Nepal, individual awareness remains critical. Schools, colleges, and community programs must include digital literacy and cyber safety education as part of their curriculum."

A Safer Digital Nepal Is Possible

Digital Nepal holds great promise, but its dark side - filled with scams, frauds, and exploitation - must not be ignored. By staying informed, cautious, and proactive, every citizen can contribute to making Nepal's digital space safer for everyone.

Stay smart. Stay safe. Stay connected.

Remember: "Your data is valuable. Protect it like cash"
