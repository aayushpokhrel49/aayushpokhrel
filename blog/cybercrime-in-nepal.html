Cybercrime in Nepal: Are We Prepared for the Growing Threats

Aayush Pokhrel
July 2, 2025
8 min read

In recent years, Nepal has seen the rapid expansion of internet usage and digital connectivity. Along with it, cybercrime has significantly increased. From online banking frauds, social media scams to large scale data breaches and online harassment, cybercriminals are exploiting the vulnerabilities of Nepal's digital infrastructure.

A Significant Rise in Cyber Incidents

19,730
Cybercrime cases reported in 2023-24

In 2023-24, the Nepal Police Cyber Bureau reported 19,730 cases, more than doubling from 9,013 the previous year, with online fraud (4,112 cases) and hacking (52% of total cases) leading the surge. This escalation mirrors global trends, as internet penetration reached 49.6% of Nepal's population (15.4 million users) in January 2024.

Common cybercrimes include phishing, identity theft, ransomware, and online harassment, with platforms like Telegram and WhatsApp frequently exploited. Common scams range from romance scams to phishing attacks, and online blackmail. Just recently, the country witnessed the exposure of a 50 GB data leak involving the Hello Sarkar grievance redressal system, highlighting how even government systems are not immune.

Why Is Nepal So Vulnerable?

1. Low Public Awareness

A large portion of the population is unaware of basic cyber hygiene practices like strong passwords, two-factor authentication, and safe online behavior. This lack of awareness makes individuals easy targets for scammers and hackers.

2. Weak Legal Framework

Nepal's cyber laws still rely heavily on the Electronic Transaction Act 2008, which is outdated and lacks provisions to address modern threats like ransomware, identity theft, and data privacy violations.

2008
Electronic Transaction Act last updated

3. Cross-Border Nature of Cybercrime

Most cyberattacks originate from outside Nepal, making prosecution difficult without international cooperation.

4. Limited Human Resources

The Nepal Police Cyber Bureau is severely under resourced. With a limited number of personnel dedicated to IT investigation, the bureau struggles to keep pace with the rapidly increasing caseload.

5. Weak Institutional Capacity

Bodies like the Nepal CERT (Computer Emergency Response Team) exist but struggle with limited budgets, lack of advanced tools and overlapping mandates.

Are We Prepared for Growth Threats?

Not actually. Despite multiple efforts underway being made, the reality is we are not ready. Nepal's digital transformation has opened up countless opportunities but with it comes a new battlefield that demands vigilance, skills, and strong systems. If current gaps are not addressed, the consequences could be severe: financial losses, erosion of trust in digital services, and a slowdown of the country's digital economy. Experts warn that Nepal remains critically underprepared to handle sophisticated cyberattacks.

Critical Vulnerability

Nepal's cybersecurity infrastructure is currently unprepared for sophisticated attacks that could target critical infrastructure like banking systems, government databases, and power grids.

It's clear that the threat is real, but the question remains "Are we prepared?" The answer lies in how seriously we act now before the next big cyber attack makes headlines.

What Should Be Done

To truly protect the nation from cyber threats, Nepal needs a multi-pronged approach:

1. Update Cyber Laws

Introduce new legislation to address modern forms of cybercrime, data privacy, digital evidence handling, AI fraud, ransomware and crypto scams.

2. Invest in Human Capital

Develop more cybersecurity professionals through dedicated training, scholarships, and incentives to retain talent in the country.

3. Establish a National Cybersecurity Authority

Create a centralized agency with clear authority to coordinate defense, monitor threats, and quick response team to help victims.

4. Public Awareness Campaigns

Launch nationwide campaigns to educate citizens about safe online practices, password security, and scam detection.

5. Strengthen International Cooperation

Collaborate with global cybersecurity experts and organizations to share threat intelligence and adopt best practices.

5 Key
Areas needing immediate attention to combat cybercrime

Cybercrime is no longer a distant threat; it's here, and it's growing. Nepal cannot afford to lag behind in this digital arms race. Without urgent reforms, stronger legal protections, better-trained personnel, and widespread public education, the country will remain vulnerable to increasingly sophisticated cyber threats.

"In the digital age, national security is as much about cybersecurity as it is about physical borders."

It's time for Nepal to recognize cybercrime as a critical national issue and act accordingly.
